1. Knee to Chest

  • 1. Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor.
  • 2. Bring one knee to your chest, helping yourself with the hands under your thigh.
  • 3. Return to the starting position and repeat with the same leg.

2. Heel Slide

  • 1. Lie on your back with your knees bent and your back in a neutral position (halfway between flat back and arched back).
  • 2. Engage your core by recruiting your pelvic floor and transverse abdominis.
  • 3. Maintain a steady abdominal breathing while you slide one foot out until your leg is straight.
  • 4. Return slowly to the initial position and repeat with the other leg.
  • 5. Your back must stay in neutral at all times.

3. Extension Lunge

  • 1. Stand up with your feet together and take a step forward with the good leg.
  • 2. The affected leg must stay planted on the ground with the heel down.

4. Stretching Quadriceps

  • 1. Lie on your side with your injured leg up and your bottom knee bent in front of you to protect your back.
  • 2. Reach back and grasp your ankle of your injured leg and gently pull towards your buttocks.
  • 3. Move your knee back without arching your back until you feel a stretch in the front of your thigh.
  • 4. Keep your knee as low to the floor as possible and maintain the position.
  • 5. You can use a towel or belt around your ankle to pull it towards your buttocks.
